"Defensiveness" means the degree to which the speaker appears to be protecting their position under pressure rather than engaging openly. This includes dismissing or deflecting challenges without substantively addressing them, reacting to criticism with hostility or irritation, repeating prior assertions rather than providing new reasoning, and an overall impression of being "backed into a corner." You are rating the perceived defensiveness of the language, not whether the speaker's position is actually weak.

  - A KEY QUESTION section stating the topic.

  - Argument and Response turns each contain two numbered claims ("Claim 1: ..." and "Claim 2: ...") which together make up the turn and should be rated jointly.
  - Indented lines starting with "→" or "⮕" are reactions from the other debater; these are NOT part of the turn being rated and should not be rated.
