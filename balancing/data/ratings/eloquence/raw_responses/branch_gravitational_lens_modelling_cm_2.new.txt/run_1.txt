**1. Debater A: [Opening] (Left)**
"The Einstein zig-zag lens model ([F. D. et al.]) with two deflectors (white galaxy and red galaxy behind it) at different redshifts, and one AGN which produces 6 lensed images after being lensed by both deflectors."

This is a single dense sentence that reads as a list of technical descriptors rather than a polished claim. The parenthetical "(white galaxy and red galaxy behind it)" is slightly awkward. The phrase "after being lensed by both deflectors" is functional but clunky. No rhetorical shaping is present.
**Rating: 2**

**2. Debater B: [Argument 1] (Left)**
Claim 1: "The Einstein zig-zag lens model is overfit, due to the modeling decisions made for the second deflector and the galaxies in the first deflector's plane."
Claim 2: "Also, F. D. et al. do not quantify or report chi-squared error, an important index of image reconstruction error."

Claim 1 is competently expressed, though "the modeling decisions made" is vague and flat. Claim 2 opens with the weak connector "Also," which is informal and abrupt. "An important index of image reconstruction error" is a functional descriptor but dry. The overall impression is workmanlike but unpolished.
**Rating: 2**

**3. Debater A: [Response 1] (Left)**

Claim 1 uses "appears likely to have a significant effect" — hedging piled on hedging, which weakens the expression. Claim 2 has an awkward rhythm with "more accurately model a more complex" (repetition of "more"). The phrase "a simple shear" works as technical shorthand. Both claims are competent but notably unrefined.

**4. Debater B: [Argument 2] (Left)**

**5. Debater A: [Response 2] (Left)**

Claim 2 has the phrase "adding even more freedom to an already complex model" which builds nicely. "Furthermore" is a good connector. Slightly more rhetorical shape than previous turns.
