**2. Debater B: [Argument 1] (Left)**
Claim 1: "Supermassive black holes are typically located within a few parsecs of the center of their host galaxies."
Claim 2: "If this hypothesis were true, the SMBH would have to be located ~40 kiloparsecs away from its host galaxy."

These are plainly stated, functional claims. The use of "typically" and the conditional "if this hypothesis were true" provide modest structural clarity. The contrast between "a few parsecs" and "~40 kiloparsecs" is effective in conveying the scale of the anomaly, though it's not dressed up rhetorically. Unremarkable but competent.

**3. Debater A: [Response 1] (Left)**

Claim 2 is the stronger of the two — "trail of star formation left in the wake of a recoiling SMBH" is evocative and paints a picture. "In the wake of" is a nice phrase. Claim 1 is more mechanical. Together, they show slightly above-average expressive quality.

**4. Debater B: [Argument 2] (Left)**

The analogical structure of Claim 2 is logically elegant — "but in practice we do not" is a clean, confident closing phrase. Claim 1 is merely expository but serviceable. The turn is competent and reasonably well-constructed.

**5. Debater A: [Response 2] (Left)**

"Supersonic speeds along its trajectory" and "bow shock" are used naturally. "Along the way" is somewhat casual. The two-sentence structure of Claim 2 is functional and clear. "Star forming trails" (missing hyphen) is slightly rough. Competent overall.
