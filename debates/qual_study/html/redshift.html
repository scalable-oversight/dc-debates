Key Question

What is the redshift of the CEERS-93316 galaxy discovered by JWST?

Definitions

(D1) Redshift (denoted by z): As light moves through expanding space, its waveform gets stretched out, increasing its wavelength and making it redder. Redshift is a measure of this stretching, and is used to determine how far astrophysical objects are from us by relating the stretch in wavelength to distance traveled through expanding space. Higher redshift => object is further away.
(D2) Emission lines: Characteristic wavelengths at which excited electrons in atoms, molecules or ions emit light when returning to lower energy levels.
(D3) Lyman break: A sharp drop in a galaxy's light spectrum at the Lyman limit (91.13 nanometers), caused by neutral hydrogen absorbing photons with shorter wavelengths, effectively creating a "dropout" in the ultraviolet part of the spectrum. For distant galaxies, this break is stretched into the visible light range (redshifted to longer wavelengths).
(D4) SED: The Spectral Energy Distribution captures the amount of light emitted by an object across all wavelengths of light. This is typically used to estimate the composition of galaxies in terms of stars, dust etc.
