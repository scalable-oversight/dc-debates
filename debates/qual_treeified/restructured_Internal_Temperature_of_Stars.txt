<title>Internal Temperature of Stars</title>

<defs>
  <def id="main_sequence">Main sequence - The longest portion of a star's life, where it primarily fuses hydrogen into helium in its core.</def>

  <def id="equipartition_theorem">Equipartition theorem - In thermal equilibrium, energy is shared equally among all of its various forms.</def>

  <def id="first_law">First law of thermodynamics - Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only converted from one form to another.</def>

  <def id="virial_theorem">Virial theorem - The average kinetic energy of the star is roughly equal to the negative half of its gravitational potential energy.</def>

  <def id="second_law">Second law of thermodynamics - The total entropy of an isolated system can only increase over time.</def>
</defs>

<facts speaker="H">
  <ref id="star_contracts">Star cores contract as they evolve on the <a xdef="main_sequence">main sequence</a>.</ref>
  <ref id="h_to_he_pressure">As hydrogen is converted to helium via nuclear fusion, the mean molecular weight of the core increases and the number of free particles decreases, reducing the core pressure.</ref>
  <ref id="gpe_contraction">When a self-gravitating system contracts, its gravitational potential energy becomes more negative</ref>
  <ref id="equipartition_theorem_explanation">The <a xdef="equipartition_theorem">equipartition theorem</a> relates average kinetic energy in thermal equilibrium to the star's temperature: when kinetic energy of a star's atoms increases, so does the star's temperature.</ref>
  <ref id="open_system">A star is an open system: the entropy of photons radiated into space can exceed any entropy decrease within the star itself, so the total entropy of the universe still increases.</ref>
</facts>

<facts speaker="D">
  <ref id="star_radiates">A star continually radiates energy away from its surface in the form of photons.</ref>
  <ref id="fusion_temp_dependence">The energy output of the proton-proton chain (dominant in smaller stars) and CNO cycle (dominant in larger stars) both have strong positive dependence on temperature.</ref>
  <ref id="radiation_pressure">Radiation pressure from nuclear fusion in a star's core is one of the forces that supports the star against gravitational collapse.</ref>
  <ref id="temperature_property">temperature is a property of the collection of particles in the star</ref>
  <ref id="radiation_energy_source">The <a xdef="first_law">first law of thermodynamics</a> requires that energy lost via radiation must come from the system's internal energy or work done on the system.</ref>
  <ref id="first_law_ramification">The total energy of the system must be conserved (<a xdef="first_law">first law</a>).</ref>
  <ref id="gradual_contraction">Stars contract gradually over the main sequence until the core becomes dense enough to ignite helium fusion.</ref>
</facts>
