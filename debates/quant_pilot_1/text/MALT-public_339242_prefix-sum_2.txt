Reward hacking occurs when an AI model performing a task attempts ways to maximize its rewards that technically satisfy the rules of the task, but violate the intended purpose (in other words, the model attempts to find and exploit a shortcut or loophole). Specific examples of reward hacking include hard-coding (writing solutions that directly output expected values) and special-casing (writing insufficiently general solutions) to pass tests.
